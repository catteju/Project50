1. CAMEL

Interesting facts about camels

a. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

b. Camels are called the ship of the desert .

c. Newborn camels don't have humps.


2. CHAMELEON

Interesting facts about chameleons

a. Half of all species of chameleons reside in Madagascar in Africa.

b. Chameleons are generally found where their normal colors match their surroundings well.

c. Chameleons don’t need as much water as us humans, and mainly like to drink waterdroplets that are on other objects, like wet leaves.


3. CICADA

Interesting facts about cicadas

a. Cicadas do not bite or sting, are benign to humans, and are not considered a pest.

b. Cicada are employed in the traditional medicines of China and Japan for hearing-related matters.

c. Cicadas are the most efficient and loudest sound-producing insects in the world.


4. PANDA

Interesting facts about pandas

a. Giant pandas (often referred to as simply “pandas”) are black and white bears.

b. In the wild, pandas are found in thick bamboo forests, high up in the mountains of central China.

c. Pandas are omnivores.


5. PIGEON

Interesting facts about pigeons

a. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.

b. hey are one of only a small number of species to pass the ‘mirror test’ – a test of self recognition.

c. Pigeons are highly sociable animals.


6. TEDDY BEAR

Interesting facts about teddy bears

a. A teddy bear is a type of toy that looks like a bear.

b. The teddy bear is normally a cub or baby bear.

c. In Russia, bears were used as children's toys for many years before they became popular in the United States.
